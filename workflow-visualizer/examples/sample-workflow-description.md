# Sample Workflow Description: Content Approval Process

This is an example of how a student might describe a workflow to Claude. You don't need to be this detailed on your first pass -- Claude will ask follow-up questions to fill in the gaps.

---

## The description (as a student might say it)

"So when someone on the marketing team writes a blog post, it goes through a few rounds of review before it gets published. Let me walk you through it.

First, the writer creates a draft in Google Docs. They usually work from a content brief that the content lead assigned them -- the brief has the topic, target audience, key points to hit, and SEO keywords.

Once the draft is done, the writer moves the card in our project board from 'Writing' to 'Ready for Review' and tags the content lead. The content lead reads through it and either approves it or sends it back with comments. If it needs revisions, the writer makes the changes and resubmits. This can go back and forth a couple of times.

After the content lead approves the writing, it goes to the design team. They create a header image and any in-post graphics. This usually takes a day or two. If the designer has questions about the visuals, they check with the content lead, not the writer.

Once design assets are done, the content lead does a final review of everything together -- copy plus visuals. If it all looks good, they schedule it for publish in our CMS and send a Slack message to the team channel with the publish date.

Oh, and one more thing -- if the post mentions any product features or pricing, it has to get a quick sign-off from the product team before the final review. That sometimes adds a day."

---

## What Claude would ask as follow-ups

- "What happens if the product team rejects the feature/pricing mention? Does it go back to the writer or does the content lead handle the edit?"
- "Is there a deadline or SLA for each review step, or does it just happen when people get to it?"
- "After publish, is there any follow-up step -- like social promotion or performance tracking?"
